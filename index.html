What is a Humanist Officiant

Understanding the unique approach to non-religious ceremonies

A Humanist officiant is a professional who creates and leads ceremonies—such as weddings, vow renewals, baby namings, milestone celebrations, or end-of-life memorials—that are non-religious, personalized, and centered on human values rather than spiritual or religious traditions.

Key features of a Humanist officiant:

  • Non-religious: The focus is on people, love, connection, and meaning—not faith or divine authority.
  • Personalized: Every ceremony is crafted around the individuals involved—their words, humour, style, and life journey.
  • Inclusive: Welcoming of diverse identities, backgrounds, and family structures.
  • Respectful and joyful: Balances dignity with warmth, often described as uplifting and celebratory while still honoring the significance of the moment.

To learn more about Humanist in Canada, you can visit www.humanistcanada.ca
